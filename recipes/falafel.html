Falafel

Home

Description

To make authentic, crispy falafel, it is essential to use dried, uncooked chickpeas soaked overnight, not canned chickpeas. The mixture is then pulsed with fresh herbs and spices in a food processor and deep-fried or baked

This recipe is for classic, fried falafel.

Ingridents

  • cup (about 8 oz or 225g) dried chickpeas, soaked overnight (8-24 hours)
  • 1/2 cup roughly chopped onion
  • 1 cup fresh parsley, packed
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro, packed
  • 3-4 cloves garlic
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1-2 tbsp flour or chickpea flour (for binding, if needed)
  • 1 tsp salt (or to taste)
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • Vegetable or canola oil for frying

Steps

  1. Soak the chickpeas: Place the dried chickpeas in a large bowl and cover with several inches of cold water.
  2. Add a pinch of baking soda to the water if desired to aid softening.
  3. Soak for at least 12 hours or overnight, then drain and rinse well.
  4. Process the mixture: Add the drained chickpeas, onion, parsley, cilantro, garlic, cumin, coriander, salt, black pepper, and baking soda to a food processor.
  5. Pulse the mixture until it is finely ground and resembles a coarse meal or wet sand, but do not blend into a smooth paste (which will make the falafel dense).
  6. Chill the dough: Transfer the mixture to a bowl, cover, and refrigerate for at least 1-2 hours.
  7. This helps the mixture bind and prevents the falafel from falling apart during cooking.
  8. If the mixture still feels too wet after chilling, add a tablespoon of flour and mix well.
  9. Shape the falafel: Using your hands (moistened slightly to prevent sticking), a small scoop, or a specialized falafel mold, form the mixture into small balls or patties, about 1 to 2 inches in diameter.
  10. Fry: Heat about 3 inches of oil in a deep pan or pot to a temperature of 350-375°F (175-190°C).
  11. Carefully lower the falafel balls into the hot oil in batches, being careful not to overcrowd the pan.
  12. Fry until deep golden brown and crispy, about 3-5 minutes total, turning occasionally.
  13. Drain and serve: Use a slotted spoon to transfer the cooked falafel to a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.
  14. Serve immediately with warm pita bread, tahini sauce, hummus, and fresh vegetables
